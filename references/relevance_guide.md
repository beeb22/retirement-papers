# Relevance Guide

This document helps calibrate the paper relevance filter. It's based on the team's Zotero library tags and real examples of papers they've collected.

## The team's Zotero tags

| Tag | Scope |
|-----|-------|
| benefits for older people | Public benefits, welfare payments, in-kind transfers received by older adults. E.g. housing benefit, pension credit, social care funding |
| consumption and living standards | Spending patterns, material wellbeing, poverty, consumption smoothing — specifically for older people or across the lifecycle |
| health | Health outcomes, disability, long-term care, mental health — for older workers or retirees specifically |
| pensions | Pension system design, DB vs DC plans, pension funds, contribution rates, pension reform, annuitisation |
| retirement/labour supply | Retirement decisions, retirement age, early retirement, labour force participation of older workers, job guarantee programmes for older unemployed |
| savings and wealth | Wealth accumulation and decumulation, retirement savings, 401k/ISA/pension pot behaviour, housing wealth, bequest motives |
| transfers | Intergenerational transfers — bequests, inheritances, inter vivos gifts, dynastic wealth transmission |
| fertility | Fertility rates and trends — the team has recently started including these papers |

## Borderline cases

These are the tricky ones that need Claude's judgment:

- Papers about **disability insurance** — relevant if it discusses older workers using DI as early retirement route, NOT relevant if purely about medical disability for any age
- Papers about **life expectancy/mortality** — relevant if it connects to pension redistribution, retirement timing, or older worker welfare; NOT relevant if it's purely epidemiological
- Papers about **housing wealth** — relevant if it connects to retirement or bequest motives; NOT relevant if purely about mortgage markets for young buyers
- Papers about **intergenerational transfers** — relevant if it's about bequests/inheritances and wealth distribution; borderline if it's purely about child outcomes
- Papers about **labour supply** — relevant ONLY if focused on older workers (50+); NOT relevant if about prime-age or young workers
- Papers about **fertility** — currently being included, so include any fertility paper

## What definitely NOT to include

- Papers about children's outcomes, education, early childhood
- Trade economics, tariffs, industrial policy
- Climate economics (unless specifically about financial security in old age and climate)
- Technology/innovation papers
- General macroeconomics without an aging angle
- Young worker labour supply, youth unemployment
